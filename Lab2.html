FACTS OF INDIA

About India:

  • India is the largest country in the South Asia Region, known for its adventures, history, and natural beauty.
  • It has a rich and multi-layered culture dominated by religious and spiritual themes.
  • India is home to thousands of languages and prides itself on being the largest democracy on Earth.

Facts on Geography of India:

  • India is situated in the continent of South Asia.
  • By area, India is the seventh-largest country in the world.
  • India is about 13 times bigger than the United Kingdom.
  • It has a long coastline, with the west coast bordering the Arabian Sea and the east coast bordering the Bay of Bengal.
  • "India" and "Bharat" are official short names for the Republic of India.
  • It is the second-most populous country and the most populous democracy in the world.
  • The Indus is the longest river in India, and the term "India" is an adaptation of the word "Indus."
  • There are nine postal zones in India, including eight regional zones and one functional zone.
  • India lies to the north of the equator between 6° 44′ and 35° 30′ north latitude and between 68° 7′ and 97° 25′ east longitude.
  • India is made up of 46 metropolitan cities, with 8 considered Mega-Cities including Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore, Chennai, Hyderabad, Ahmedabad, Kolkata, and Surat.
  • The international telephone code for India is +91.
  • The National Capital Region (NCR) is the world's largest agglomeration in India.
  • India consists of 29 states and seven Union territories.
  • Indian Railways is the largest employer in the world.
  • Mahatma Gandhi, known as Karamchand Uttamchand Gandhi, is the father of the nation.
  • India is bordered by Nepal, China to the northeast, Bhutan to the northwest, and Burma to the east.
  • India’s largest city is Mumbai, and its capital city is New Delhi.
  • India has a total area of 3,287,263 sq. km.
  • India's first-ever satellite, Aryabhata, was launched by the Soviet Union on April 19, 1975.

FACTS OF INDIA

About India:

  • India is the largest country in the South Asia Region, known for its adventures, history, and natural beauty.
  • It has a rich and multi-layered culture dominated by religious and spiritual themes.
  • India is home to thousands of languages and prides itself on being the largest democracy on Earth.

Facts on Geography of India:

  • India is situated in the continent of South Asia.
  • By area, India is the seventh-largest country in the world.
  • India is about 13 times bigger than the United Kingdom.
  • India has a long coastline, with the west coast bordering the Arabian Sea and the east coast bordering the Bay of Bengal.
  • India is the second-most populous country and the most populous democracy in the world.
  • The total area of India is approximately 3.287 million square kilometers.
  • India shares borders with seven countries: Pakistan, China, Nepal, Bhutan, Bangladesh, Myanmar, and Afghanistan.
  • The highest point in India is Kanchenjunga in the Himalayas, standing at 8,586 meters (28,169 feet).
  • The Thar Desert, also known as the Great Indian Desert, is one of the worldâ€™s largest arid regions and spans over 200,000 square kilometers.
  • India has several major rivers, including the Ganges, Yamuna, Brahmaputra, and Godavari, providing water and fertile soil for agriculture.
  • The country experiences diverse climates, ranging from tropical in the south to temperate and alpine in the north.
  • India has rich biodiversity, home to a wide range of flora and fauna, including endangered species like the Bengal tiger and Indian rhinoceros.
  • The Western Ghats and Eastern Ghats are two prominent mountain ranges that run along the west and east coasts of India, respectively.
  • India is divided into 28 states and 8 union territories, each with its own distinct geography, climate, and culture.
